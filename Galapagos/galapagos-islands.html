Galápagos Islands

The Galápagos Islands are an archipelago of volcanic islands by the equator in the Pacific Ocean. It is 906 km west of Ecuador. The islands and the water surrounding it make up the Galápagos Province of Ecuador, the Galápagos National Park, and the Galápagos Marine Reserve. The islands are known for having a myriad of animal species. The Galápagos Islands were studied by Charles Darwin, and because of the diverse life there, he came up with the theory of evolution. The islands have a population of about 25,000 people. The main language spoken there is Spanish.
